About Me

My name is Jaime Cernuda, I am a third year PhD student at the Scalable Computing Software Laboratory at the Illinois Institute of Technology. Originally, an electrical engineer at the Technical University of Madrid (UPM). I moved to Chicago to get my master in Computer Science in here, I work with the SCS lab since Feb-2018, after a few month working with them I decided to join the PhD program at Illinois Tech.

I work in High-Performance Computing (HPC) in the area of I/O and storage systems, specifically looking at problems involving hierarchical storage systems, single-node or cluster situations where you have more than one type of storage device, for example how to handle I/O on a node with Permanent Memory and an NVMe device or a cluster with some storage nodes containing an SSD and a greater number of them containing HDD. Concretely, my own personal research has been dedicated to exploring real-time, continuous I/O (usually referred to as Data Streaming) in these hierarchical environments with a special interest in adaptive and elastic storage systems that can change their deployment size and resource utilization based on user demand.
